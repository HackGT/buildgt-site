FAQ’s

What is a hackathon?

Hackathons are events where teams of students spend a period of time utilizing resources and support that we provide to bring their ideas to life. These projects range from mobile applications to drones to embedded hardware and more!

What if I don't have a team?

We will have a mixer at the start of the event to help you find a team! Additionally, we have a Facebook event for attendees to share their ideas and find a team before the event.

How long is the hackathon?

This year's event is from 9am to 9pm on March 10th.

Can I keep what I build?

Checked out hardware such as Raspberry Pis, Oculus Rifts, etc. are the only things you cannot keep.

What hardware will be there?

We'll be providing lots of hardware, including Arduinos, Oculus Rifts, Raspberry Pis, and more! You can find a full list of available hardware at hardware.hack.gt

How does judging work?

During the expo, projects will be judged on a variety of factors such as technical prowess, usability, and creativity.

Who can attend?

Anyone 18 years or older can attend! With the resources provided and beginner-friendly workshops, students from any background can come and enjoy!

What size should teams be?

The max team size is 4 people.

About Us

HackGT is a 501(c)(4) nonprofit and student organization consisting of Georgia Tech undergraduate students focused on being the drivers of computer science outreach in the Georgia Tech, state, and national communities. Through throwing large-scale collegiate hackathons of upwards of 1000 people to small-scale hackathons and workshops, our organization aims to spread the spirit of application, innovation, and creativity in computer science education.
